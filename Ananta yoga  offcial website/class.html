Traditional yoga

Traditional yoga practice takes a holistic, 360-degree approach to health and wellness, focusing on physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual aspects. It emphasizes the union of mind, body, and soul, creating harmony among all three.

Hatha yoga

Hatha yoga is a school of yoga that focuses on physical postures (asanas) and breath control (pranayama) to balance the body, mind, and spirit, and is often considered an umbrella term for many common yoga styles practiced in the West. It involves slow, deliberate movements, holding poses, and mindfulness, aiming to improve flexibility, strength, balance, and relaxation.

Rubber Neti (Kriya)

Rubber Neti, also known as Sutra Neti, is a yogic practice involving inserting a rubber tube or cord into the nasal passages to cleanse the nasal tract. It is used to clear blockages, remove mucus, and improve nasal hygiene.

Pranayam

Pranayama is a yogic practice focused on regulating breath to influence the flow of prana, or life force, within the body. It involves controlled inhalation, exhalation, and breath retention, aiming to connect the body and mind and promote overall well-being.

Power Yoga

Power Yoga is a physically intense style of Vinyasa yoga, emphasizing strength, flexibility, and endurance through flowing sequences and synchronized breathing. It builds upon the Ashtanga yoga system but offers more creative and diverse sequences.

Surya Namaskar

Surya Namaskar, also known as Sun Salutation, is a sequence of yoga poses that originated in India and is widely practiced as a form of exercise and spiritual practice. It consists of a series of 12 linked asanas, which are traditionally performed to greet the sun and express gratitude for its life-sustaining energy. The practice is deeply rooted in Indian culture and Hinduism, where the sun is revered as a deity and the source of all life

Meditation

Meditation is a practice that involves focusing or clearing the mind to achieve a state of calm, clarity, and well-being. It can be done using various techniques, including focusing on the breath, a mantra, or a visualization. Regular meditation can bring numerous benefits, such as reduced stress and anxiety, improved focus, and enhanced emotional regulation.
